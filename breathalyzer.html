Breathalyzer Usability for Clinical Trials

Critical tasks • Providers & participants • Learnability • Workload

Task AnalysisUMUX-LiteNASA-TLXFormative Study

Objective

Ensure intended users can safely and effectively perform setup, sample collection, result readout, and data handoff with acceptable workload and satisfaction.

Method

  • Defined critical tasks & error taxonomy; observed time-on-task and assistance
  • Collected UMUX-Lite (usability) and NASA-TLX (workload)
  • Prioritized mitigations for labeling, affordances, training media

Key findings

  • Label & connector tweaks reduced setup errors on first attempt
  • Brief video + quick-start card lowered workload and increased success
  • Acceptance thresholds & trial-readiness checklist delivered

Task flow   Measurement plan

Users: providers administering tests; participants submitting samples
Outcomes: critical-task success, error types, time, assistance, UMUX-Lite, NASA-TLX
Artifacts: labeling spec, procedure card, training script/video
