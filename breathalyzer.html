Breathalyzer Usability — Formative & Summative Evaluation

MedTech • Critical tasks • Providers & participants • Learnability • Workload

Formative UsabilitySummative Validation Task AnalysisUMUX-LiteNASA-TLX

Objective

Ensure intended users (providers administering tests; participants submitting samples) can safely and effectively complete setup, sample collection, result readout, and data handoff with acceptable workload and satisfaction to support trial readiness.

Approach

  • Formative (exploratory): contextual inquiry and task analysis to map critical paths and error modes; rapid prototyping of labels, microcopy, and assembly affordances.
  • Summative (validation): structured task scenarios with success criteria and acceptance thresholds; quantitative instruments (UMUX-Lite, NASA-TLX) and error taxonomy scoring.

Measures

  • Primary: critical-task success rate, first-attempt success, error types/severity, time-on-task, assistance
  • Secondary: UMUX-Lite (perceived usability), NASA-TLX (workload), post-task confidence

Key Findings

  • Labeling and connector affordances reduced setup errors on first attempt.
  • Short training video + pocket procedure card lowered workload and improved success for novices.
  • Defined acceptance thresholds and a trial-readiness checklist for handoff to PI and operations.

Impact

  • Evidence-based changes to labeling, assembly order, and quick-start guidance.
  • Validated the device workflow against acceptance criteria for clinical trial use.

Task flow   Measurement plan

Highlights:
