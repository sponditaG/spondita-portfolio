Overview

As a mixed-methods UX researcher, I led a two-phase study to improve how cognitively normal older adults interpret a 5-year Alzheimer’s disease risk report. I partnered with my PI and researchers to translate complex model outputs into usable, trustworthy communication. The work spanned discovery (qualitative) and validation (quantitative) phases and directly informed an evidence-based redesign.

Role

  • Owned research plan across discovery & validation; created IRB-aligned materials
  • Facilitated stakeholder sessions to align on intent (risk ≠ absolute number)
  • Operationalized insights into testable design hypotheses and prototypes

Phase 1 — Discovery (Qualitative)

Methods: Cognitive interviews, think-alouds, contextual inquiry, thematic analysis

  • Risk number understanding: Participants treated probabilities as certainties
  • Terminology friction: “Developing” vs. “not developing” AD created confusion
  • Information load: Text-heavy layout increased cognitive burden; weak visual hierarchy
  • Model transparency: Questions about what inputs drive the risk model (biomarkers, demographics)

Partnered with researchers who emphasized communicating risk as probabilistic, not deterministic—this guided our design hypotheses for visual and verbal reframing.

Phase 2 — Validation (Quantitative A/B)

Methods: Survey-based A/B testing of graphical (icon arrays, bar/dot charts) and verbal framings

  • Measured comprehension and recall of risk information
  • Captured emotional response (worry/reassurance)
  • Collected format preference (visual vs. text)
  • Recorded numeracy and demographics to analyze correlations with understanding

Key Findings

  • Icon arrays and simplified charts improved comprehension & recall vs. text-only
  • Plain-language reframing (e.g., “memory and thinking problems”) reduced ambiguity
  • Adding a clear “what to do next” section increased perceived usefulness and trust

Impact & Deliverables

  • Redesigned report template with visual hierarchy, icon arrays, and plain-language guidance
  • Participant FAQ and clinician script to support consistent conversations
  • Evidence pack: protocol, measures, insight summary, and recommendations for rollout
